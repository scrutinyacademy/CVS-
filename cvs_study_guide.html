🫀 The Cardiovascular System — Simply Put

Think of the heart as a powerful pump that never stops working — not even for a second!

❤️ What is the CVS?

The Cardiovascular System (CVS) = Heart + Blood Vessels (Arteries, Veins, Capillaries). Its job is simple: deliver oxygen & nutrients to every cell and carry away waste (CO₂, urea).

🌟 Simple analogy: Imagine your body is a city. The heart is the water pump, arteries are the supply pipes, veins are the drainage pipes, and capillaries are the tiny lanes connecting every house (cell).

RA = Right Atrium | LA = Left Atrium | RV = Right Ventricle | LV = Left Ventricle

🩸 Blood Circuits

Pulmonary Circuit:
Heart → Lungs → Heart
(for oxygenation)


Systemic Circuit:
Heart → Body → Heart
(for delivering O₂)

💡 Right side pumps deoxygenated blood to lungs. Left side pumps oxygenated blood to body.

🫁 Blood Vessels

VesselKey Feature
ArteriesCarry blood AWAY from heart; thick wall
VeinsCarry blood TO heart; valves present

⚡ Heart Physiology

How the heart beats, pumps, and regulates itself — made simple.

⚡ Cardiac Action Potential — The Electrical Story

The heart generates its own electricity. This is called automaticity or autorhythmicity.

📍 Pacemaker of the heart = SA Node (Sinoatrial Node) — located in right atrium
Rate: 60–100 bpm | Inherent rate without any nerve input = 100 bpm
Vagal tone normally slows it to 60–100 bpm

Conduction pathway (think of electricity spreading):

→
SA NodeGenerates impulse (pacemaker)
→
AV NodeDelays impulse (0.1 sec) — gives atria time to fill ventricles
→
Bundle of HisSingle pathway down the septum
→
Left & Right Bundle BranchesSpreads to both ventricles
→
Purkinje FibersFinal delivery to ventricular muscle → contraction!

💓 Cardiac Cycle

One complete heartbeat = Systole + Diastole


🔴 Systole = Ventricles contract → blood ejected
Duration: ~0.3 sec


🔵 Diastole = Ventricles relax → blood fills in
Duration: ~0.5 sec

Total cycle = 0.8 sec at 75 bpm
💡 Diastole is longer than systole — heart rests more!

📐 Cardiac Output — The Big Formula

CO = Heart Rate × Stroke Volume
5000 mL/min = 70 bpm × 70 mL

Factors affecting Stroke Volume (Starling's Law):

  • Preload ↑ → SV ↑ (more blood stretches ventricle → stronger contraction)
  • Afterload ↑ → SV ↓ (more resistance = harder to eject)
  • Contractility ↑ → SV ↑ (sympathetic stimulation, adrenaline)
🎯 Frank-Starling Law: "The heart pumps out what comes in." More preload → more stretch → more force. Like a rubber band!

🧠 ANS Control of Heart

⬆️ Sympathetic (Fight or Flight)

  • ↑ Heart Rate (positive chronotropy)
  • ↑ Contractility (positive inotropy)
  • ↑ Conduction (positive dromotropy)
  • Neurotransmitter: Noradrenaline

⬇️ Parasympathetic (Rest & Digest)

  • ↓ Heart Rate (negative chronotropy)
  • ↓ Contractility (negative inotropy)
  • ↓ Conduction speed
  • Neurotransmitter: Acetylcholine

🎵 Heart Sounds — Lub Dub Explained

S1 — "LUB" 🔊

Caused by closure of Mitral & Tricuspid valves (AV valves)
Marks beginning of Systole
Best heard: Apex (5th ICS, MCL)

S2 — "DUB" 🔊

Caused by closure of Aortic & Pulmonary valves (Semilunar)
Marks beginning of Diastole
Best heard: Base of heart (2nd ICS)

💡 Memory trick: S1 = "All" valves open → AV valves close (Mitral + Tricuspid)
S2 = Aortic + Pulmonary (Semilunar) valves close

Extra SoundWhenMeaning
S3 "Ken-tuc-KY"Early diastoleVentricular gallop — Heart failure / young normal
S4 "TEN-nes-SEE"Late diastole (pre-systolic)Atrial gallop — Stiff ventricle (hypertension, HOCM)
⚠️ S3 in adults → almost always pathological (Heart Failure). S3 in young (under 30) → may be normal!

💡 Pacemaker Hierarchy — "Some Big Punks Venture Fast"

SASA Node — 60–100 bpm (fastest = pacemaker)
AVAV Node — 40–60 bpm (escape rhythm if SA fails)
BBundle of His — 40 bpm
PPurkinje Fibers — 20–40 bpm (last resort)
💡 Each lower pacemaker is slower. If SA fails, AV takes over. If AV fails, bundle takes over — this is called escape rhythm.
